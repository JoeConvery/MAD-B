The Bad: Explored

In a time like the one we live in, where the wealth disparity is growing, jobs are increasingly hard to come by, and the cost of living keeps increasing, nobody wants to lose their livelyhood to a machine. AI art algorithms taking jobs however is particularly vexxing as these are jobs people love. Cashiers will morn their loss of income but probably not their job, and this could be solved with better economic policies like UBI, but artists live for their craft.


In 2022 a participant won the Colorado State Fair's art competition with a piece of AI art. Not only did this take the prize money from someone who put real effort and dedication into their work, it also took any recognition and further work that they might have gained from winning the title.

The other participants weren't the only people this winner stole from however. "Midjourney" was the program used to generate the image, so any pieces of art this AI model scraped to train and create the winning piece have also been stolen from.


Legally, whether AI art counts as theft is still being worked out in the courts, but the public consensus does seem in favour of it being so. This was given a bump in support when people started discovering what appeared to be signatures in pieces of AI generated art. Even if this is just the AI immitating sigantures, it still proves that it has been fed signed work and it seems doubtful that the creator of such works would do this willingly.
